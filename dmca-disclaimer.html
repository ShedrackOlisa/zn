Reporting Copyright Issues

If you believe we're hosting content that infringes your copyright, please let us know. We comply with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) and will respond to all valid infringement notices.

Before you contact us: You might want to consult with a lawyer first, as falsely claiming copyright infringement can have legal consequences.

What we need from you:

To file a copyright complaint, please provide:

  1. Proof that you're authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner
  2. Detailed identification of the copyrighted work (include at least one search term that finds it on our site)
  3. A statement that you believe the use isn't authorized by the copyright owner
  4. A statement that your notice is accurate (under penalty of perjury)
  5. Your contact information

What Happens Next

When we receive a valid DMCA notice:

  1. We'll promptly remove or disable access to the content in question
  2. We'll notify the user who posted the content
  3. If appropriate, we may suspend or terminate repeat infringers' accounts

If you believe we made a mistake

If you think we removed your content by error, you can file a counter-notification with:

  1. Your contact information
  2. Identification of the removed content
  3. A statement under penalty of perjury that the removal was mistaken
