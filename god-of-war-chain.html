The Origins of Kratos’ Vengeance

Set before the events of the original God of War game, Chains of Olympus serves as a prequel that delves into Kratos’ servitude to the gods of Olympus. The narrative explores his darker days as a Spartan warrior under the command of the gods, offering a more intimate and tragic portrayal of the Ghost of Sparta.

The story opens with Kratos battling Persian invaders in the city of Attica. As always, things quickly escalate from standard warfare to mythological madness. He’s soon thrown into a divine conspiracy involving the sudden disappearance of the sun god Helios, plunging the world into eternal darkness. What follows is a soul-wrenching journey where Kratos must travel through realms like the Underworld and battle figures such as Persephone and Charon, all while confronting his own haunted past.

Unlike many action games that just throw in a narrative as an afterthought, Chains of Olympus weaves its story tightly into its gameplay. Every mythological element is grounded in its lore, making each twist feel earned and emotionally impactful.

You’ll be swinging, slashing, juggling, and finishing off enemies with brutal flair. Each animation is smooth, and every hit feels crunchy and consequential.
