Unlocked later in the game, Wildvine is great for long-range vine attacks and swinging from plant hooks. He adds a platforming twist to traversal and is essential in certain levels filled with tricky jumps and hidden paths.

Combat and Gameplay Mechanics

The combat system in Ben 10: Protector of Earth is straightforward but satisfying. It’s a mix of light and heavy attacks, combos, aerial strikes, and alien-specific powers. As you battle enemies, you collect energy that refuels the Omnitrix. This energy allows you to transform and stay in alien form longer. Once it runs out, you’re back to Ben — a much weaker version, so managing your meter becomes part of the strategy.

You’ll also pick up DNA points and special collectibles that let you upgrade your aliens. These upgrades improve damage, unlock new moves, and increase your Omnitrix meter. It’s not an RPG, but there is light progression that gives you a reason to explore every corner and complete every fight.

The enemy variety is decent, ranging from Vilgax’s drones to mutated bugs, alien tech monsters, and mini-bosses. Each has different attack patterns, requiring you to choose the right alien for the job. Some enemies need to be stunned before you can deal real damage, while others might be vulnerable to ranged attacks or area-of-effect moves.
