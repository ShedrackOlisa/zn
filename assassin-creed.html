What Is Assassin’s Creed Bloodlines?

Released in 2009 exclusively for the PlayStation Portable (PSP), Assassin's Creed Bloodlines serves as a direct sequel to the first Assassin's Creed game. It follows Altaïr Ibn-La'Ahad as he journeys from the Holy Land to the island of Cyprus, continuing his battle against the Templars and further exploring his own beliefs and identity as an assassin.

This isn't just a side story. Bloodlines adds genuine narrative weight to the series and offers an intimate look into Altaïr’s path of redemption, his evolving ideology, and how he handles his growing role within the Assassin Brotherhood. While it’s optimized for the portable platform, it still delivers signature Assassin’s Creed stealth, parkour, and historical intrigue.

2. Parkour Movement System

Altaïr remains the agile, graceful assassin we remember. Bloodlines maintains the parkour mechanics, allowing you to:

Combat in Bloodlines is fluid and deeply satisfying. Altaïr comes equipped with:

The game is set on the Mediterranean island of Cyprus, specifically in the cities of Limassol and Kyrenia. Each area is filled with detail, secrets, and enemy outposts. Despite being on PSP, the cities feel alive with bustling crowds, guards on patrol, and landmarks to scale.

Unlike the open-world vastness of the console titles, Bloodlines leans more into semi-open level design. This makes it ideal for quick sessions on the go without sacrificing depth or replayability.

Storyline and Narrative Themes

Assassin’s Creed Bloodlines continues right after the events of the original Assassin’s Creed. Altaïr, having defeated Al Mualim and uncovered the Templar’s grand conspiracy, sets sail for Cyprus to hunt down the remaining Templar leaders who fled the Holy Land.

Throughout his journey, Altaïr uncovers more about the Templar Order’s expansion, their manipulation of local rulers, and the early signs of the ideological war that would span centuries.

He also meets Maria Thorpe, a Templar agent with whom he shares a complex relationship. Their dynamic evolves over the course of the game, laying the foundation for future events in the series. If you’ve played later entries, you know just how pivotal this relationship becomes to the lore.

Key Narrative Themes

  • Redemption: Altaïr is no longer the arrogant young assassin from the first game. Bloodlines shows his journey toward becoming a leader and a philosopher of the Creed.
  • Control vs Freedom: The game deepens the central theme of the Assassin-Templar conflict—whether peace can be achieved through control or liberty.
  • Trust: Altaïr must decide whom he can trust as he navigates new territory, deceptive allies, and cunning enemies.
