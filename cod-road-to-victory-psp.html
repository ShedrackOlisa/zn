The World War II Setting Reimagined for Handheld Play

From the very beginning, Call of Duty has been deeply rooted in the World War II era, and Road to Victory proudly continues that tradition. What’s unique about this title is that it doesn’t try to overcomplicate things. Instead, it brings the intensity of the battlefield to a handheld format, focusing on three major Allied forces — the Americans, the British, and the Canadians — and their separate but intertwined roles in the fight against Axis powers.

The campaign is broken into distinct chapters, each following one of the Allied nations. Each campaign offers its own set of unique missions that reflect real-life operations, battlefield conditions, and squad-based objectives. The atmosphere is soaked in the historical tension of WWII, from the dusty ruins of European cities to chaotic beach landings and covert night raids.

Three Campaigns, One Unified Goal

The game is structured across three main perspectives:

  • American Campaign: You'll find yourself storming beaches, clearing bunkers, and defending positions under heavy fire. This is the more direct and action-packed set of missions, designed to keep you constantly moving and shooting.
  • British Campaign: More stealth and strategy-focused, British missions often rely on silenced weapons, behind-enemy-lines operations, and precise coordination with allies.
  • Canadian Campaign: Perhaps the most balanced of the three, the Canadian missions blend fast-paced assaults with tactical challenges like defending bridges or holding off counterattacks.

Each campaign brings new challenges, new scenery, and a new reason to stay glued to your screen. While they don’t feature a deeply cinematic story, the mission objectives, voice acting, and battle sounds all combine to create an immersive war zone you’ll want to return to.

Mission Variety That Keeps You Hooked

One of the best things about Road to Victory is the way it structures its missions. You're not just running and gunning in every level. Instead, the game breaks up its pacing with a smart variety of gameplay elements:

  • Infiltration missions where stealth is key, and being spotted means restarting the level.
  • Defensive stand-offs where you must hold a line against waves of enemies until reinforcements arrive.
  • Demolition objectives where you plant charges and escape before everything blows up.
  • Sniper missions that zoom into precision aiming under intense conditions.

The beauty of this variety is that it never feels repetitive. Each mission feels handcrafted with a different goal and environment in mind, forcing you to adapt and experiment with different weapons and tactics. Whether you're crawling under barbed wire while bullets whiz over your head or sprinting through a ruined church to save pinned-down allies, there's always something new happening.

Weapons of War – Authentic and Effective

Authenticity is something this game does well, especially when it comes to its weapon selection. You’ll find a solid lineup of WWII-era firearms and explosives, each with distinct handling and impact. Here’s a look at some of the weapons you’ll be using throughout the game:

  • M1 Garand: Semi-automatic rifle with solid range and accuracy.
  • Thompson SMG: A fast-firing close-range beast used by American troops.
  • Sten Gun: The British go-to SMG — quiet, quick, and reliable.
  • Lee-Enfield: A bolt-action rifle for those high-damage, slower-paced shootouts.
  • Kar98k: Used by Axis forces, but you’ll often pick it up off enemies for sniping.
  • Grenades and Satchel Charges: Perfect for clearing trenches or blowing up key objectives.

The gunplay feels rewarding, especially considering the limitations of handheld hardware. Reload animations, recoil feedback, and firing sounds all feel punchy. Every time you land a headshot or lob a grenade into an enemy nest, there's that satisfying jolt of impact you expect from the Call of Duty franchise.

Combat and AI Design

The enemies in Road to Victory aren’t just standing around waiting to be shot. They’ll duck behind cover, move between positions, and even attempt to flank you when you’re not paying attention. The AI isn’t revolutionary, but it’s good enough to make firefights feel alive. Enemies react to grenades, use machine guns to pin you down, and call for reinforcements if the battle drags on.

As for your allies, they do a decent job of holding the line, but don’t expect them to carry the mission — that job’s yours. You’ll often need to lead the charge, provide cover fire, or outmaneuver enemies when your AI squadmates are overwhelmed.
