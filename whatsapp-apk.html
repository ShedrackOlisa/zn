History of WhatsApp

WhatsApp was founded in 2009 by Brian Acton and Jan Koum, two former Yahoo! employees. The app was initially created as a way to send messages between friends, but it quickly gained popularity and became a global phenomenon.

How WhatsApp Works

WhatsApp uses a combination of internet and cellular networks to send messages, make calls, and share files. The app uses end-to-end encryption to ensure that messages are secure and private.

WhatsApp Business

WhatsApp Business is a version of the app designed specifically for businesses. It offers a range of features, including the ability to create a business profile, send messages to customers, and track analytics.

WhatsApp Pay

WhatsApp Pay is a payment feature that allows users to send and receive money directly within the app. This feature is currently available in several countries, including India, Brazil, and Mexico.

WhatsApp Web

WhatsApp Web is a web-based version of the app that allows users to access their WhatsApp account from their computer. This feature is useful for those who want to use WhatsApp on a larger screen or for those who prefer typing on a keyboard.

WhatsApp Security

WhatsApp takes security seriously, with end-to-end encryption on all messages, calls, and files shared through the app. This means that only the sender and recipient can read the content of the message, and not even WhatsApp can access it.

WhatsApp Privacy

WhatsApp is committed to protecting user privacy, with features like two-factor authentication and the ability to control who can contact you. Users can also choose to delete their account at any time, which will permanently remove all of their data from WhatsApp servers.
